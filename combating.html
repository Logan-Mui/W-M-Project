How to combat misinformation

Introduction

The best way to combat misinformation is to keep yourself informed

You can stay informed by reading from relevant and trusted news sources

Below is a chart that shows popular news outlets and their reliability and politcal skew

Another great way to combat misinformation is to fact check your information,
a few useful websites to factcheck from are:

FactCheck.org

Factcheck.org is a nonpartisan and nonprofit organization that advocates for voters to reduce the confusion of U.S. politics by monitoring and stating the factual accuracy of the major U.S. political players in various types of media.

Politifact.com

Politifact is a journalist website that seeks to fact check journalism, they check newsworthy and significant stories and statements made on social media or otherwise and correct it.

Snopes.com

Snopes is the oldest and largest fact checking site online that links and documents it's sources so that you can even do your own independent research

Lastly, it's important to discuss the information with loved ones or people you know well. The best way for you and others to learn about what trustworthy information is typically social interactions or anecdotes from others, rather than experts. Now this isn't to say that you shouldn't listen to experts, but more to spread the word of the experts.

In trying times like a worldwide pandemic or the hot climate of politics, it's important that the information that everyone recieves is trustworthy to be safe and authentic. Which is why misinformation is such a dangerous threat to our society
